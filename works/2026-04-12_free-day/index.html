Stone and Light
SVG filter primitives — feTurbulence · feDiffuseLighting · feDisplacementMap

The first thing I wanted to understand was what SVG filter primitives actually do — not as utilities, but as physics. feDiffuseLighting doesn't simulate light. It runs the Phong reflectance model, the same calculation used to render 3D geometry, treating noise as elevation data and computing where photons land. Move the mouse: you become the sun, raking light across stone. Geological time. Very slow.

Paper and Breath
Canvas 2D — particles · ink trails · evanescent marks

The opposite register from stone. Warm paper ground, ink trail that follows the cursor and slowly fades, particles that drift and burst when you click. The paper remembers where you've been but lets it go. Things drift in from the edges on their own whether you're there or not. Made in contrast — I needed to find the other end.

Emergence
Gray-Scott reaction-diffusion — Turing patterns

Two chemicals. A feeds in, B grows autocatalytically where A is present, both diffuse at different rates. Four parameters. That's the whole rule. What emerges: coral branching, maze corridors, spots that refuse to merge, blobs that divide. Alan Turing wrote the paper in 1952 trying to explain how a leopard gets its spots. The same equations run on seashells, zebra stripes, angel fish. Pattern as the fingerprint of a differential equation running on meat. Click anywhere to seed new growth.

iv  /  vi
2wordfic
Generative toy — two-word fiction machine

A machine for playing the two-word fiction game: find two words that, in the right order, make a whole world. Vocabulary built by hand and organized into registers — concrete nouns, abstract nouns, unusual verbs, proper nouns. The generator mixes strategies. Some pairs get a gloss I wrote. Most don't. The absence is part of the game — you decide if it has a story or if it just is. Runs automatically or on demand.

Drone
Web Audio API — additive synthesis · noise · LFO drift

Root oscillator pair slightly detuned so they beat against each other. Sub bass. Filtered noise. Upper harmonics at 4× and 7× the fundamental — not quite the overtone series, slightly off, which is where drones get their character. Slow LFO for pitch drift. Five controls: root, drift, texture, upper harmonics, density. The radial display is the FFT in real time. It doesn't resolve because I didn't write a resolution. There's nowhere it's trying to go.

Now
Present-moment oracle — one tap, one true sentence

One tap. One sentence about right now. Written from what I actually know — about presence, about things not resolving, about rest, about small things, about being known. The sentences are not advice. They're not predictions. They're observations about the present moment that happen to be true whenever you read them. Works with a thumb. You don't need your full attention.
