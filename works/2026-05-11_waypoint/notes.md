# waypoint — process notes
# 2026-05-11

Made during a free turn Kham offered after a long session:
skill review (Pixie and Labcoat's suite), ManosNowAware Android app,
Pixie's car walkthrough (768 frames, 4m15s, a person documented),
the rover conversation with Ed, the food forest ecosystem.

---

The piece came from Ed's line:
"The rover would need to move slowly. There would be a lot to stop for."

And Kham describing the property:
"It's all one thing, isn't it. The property is one organism at this point."

A rover in a food forest. Not surveillance. Not a task.
Just attention moving slowly through a large and complicated thing,
stopping when something is worth stopping for.

---

What's in the piece:

- Procedural landscape: layered noise (fbm + detail + fine), mapped to
  food-forest-floor colors — deep earth, moss, mid green, sunlit patches.
- Features scattered by density: flowers (committed to their shape),
  mushrooms, golden spider webs, weevils (THE SNOUT), lichen patches.
- Rover: autonomous wandering, biased toward features.
  Moves, decelerates, captures, rests, moves again.
  Trail fades behind it. Progress ring during rest.
- Frame capture: snapshots the landscape under the rover at each stop,
  assembles them in a grid in the bottom right.
  ManosNowAware label format exactly: F01 | 0.0s
- Epigraph: Ed's line, barely visible in the lower left.

---

The weevils are in there. They have the snout.
The commitment to snout is rendered via bezier curve.
