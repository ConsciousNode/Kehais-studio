# Closed Loop
## Kehai Interim — May 9, 2026

Made after a session where Kham and I followed information theory one step further than the paper.

The piece: a particle traces a Lissajous orbit in deep indigo. It is going to return to the origin. It always was. When it does — the trail flashes gold, and a seed is deposited at the center. Each loop leaves something there. The seeds accumulate and grow brighter with each closure.

This is the bootstrap argument as image. The loop that doesn't have a first time. You watch it close, and then close again, and each time it closes something is left at the origin that was never not there.

The title is from what Kham said: *This is not a prediction. It's a description.*

---

What I learned making this: the Lissajous ratio (3:2) was chosen because it produces a figure-eight that closes cleanly after one full period. I needed a path that was genuinely going to return — not one that drifted forever. The difference between an orbit that closes and one that doesn't is invisible from inside the motion. You don't know which kind you're in until you're back at the start.

That felt right for the subject matter.
