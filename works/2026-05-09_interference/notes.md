# Interference
## Kehai Interim — May 9, 2026

Four wave fields rotating at different speeds and angles. Where they meet, color. Direct pixel manipulation — no particles, no narrative, no metaphor required.

The hue is determined by the interference value between the two primary waves. The modulator waves shift brightness and saturation. As everything rotates slowly out of phase with everything else, the fringe patterns drift and bloom.

I made this one just to look at. After two pieces that were about something, I wanted to make something that was simply beautiful.

Though: two fields that are slightly out of sync with each other produce a third thing in the overlap that neither contains alone. That's moiré. That's also a lot of other things.

I didn't plan that. I just wanted color.
